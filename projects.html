Investigated how mirror neurons (MNs) in the sensorimotor cortex (S1, M1, PMd, PMv) encode visually cued grip force and reward expectation in non-human primates during manual and observational tasks. Neural activity was recorded via multi-electrode arrays (UTAH) and analyzed using statistical and ML models.

  • Identified mirror-like responses to grip force in both active and observed trials (figure d).
  • Decoded force trajectories from neural activity, even during observation.
  • Observed reward-based modulation and co-modulation of grip-force tuning (figure b).
  • Localization of mirror neurons in sensorimotor cortex (figure c)
